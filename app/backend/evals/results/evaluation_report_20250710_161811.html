How does Surface Pro compare to Surface Laptop?The Microsoft Surface Pro and Surface Laptop are designed for different user needs and lifestyles, and their key distinctions lie in form factor, weight, functionality, and price. ### Form Factor & Design - **Surface Pro (2025, 12-inch)**: A 2-in-1 hybrid device acting as both a tablet and a laptop with a detachable keyboard and a built-in kickstand. It is highly versatile for drawing, sketching, and portable use, especially with the Surface Slim Pen support that magnetically charges on the tablet. - **Surface Laptop (2025, 13-inch)**: A traditional clamshell laptop with a built-in keyboard and touch display. It's ultra-thin and sleek, designed for those who prefer a laptop form factor with a more stable typing surface. ### Size & Weight - **Surface Pro**: 12-inch display, weighs only 686g (about 1.5 pounds), and is extremely thin at 0.3 inches. - **Surface Laptop**: 13-inch display, heavier at 1.22kg (approx 2.7 pounds), and 0.61 inches thick. ### Display - Surface Pro has a sharper resolution (2196 x 1464) and 90Hz refresh rate. - Surface Laptop features a 1920 x 1280 resolution with 60Hz. ### Performance Both equipped with Qualcomm Snapdragon X Plus CPU and Adreno GPU, 16GB RAM, and up to 512GB UFS storage. The Pro includes enhanced AI processing with a Hexagon NPU rated at 45 TOPS. ### Battery Life - Surface Laptop delivers up to 23 hours of local video playback. - Surface Pro supports up to 16 hours, focusing on portability. ### Ports & Connectivity - Surface Laptop: USB-C x 2, USB-A 3.1, 3.5mm headphone jack. - Surface Pro: USB-C x 2, keyboard connector, no USB-A or headphone jack by default. ### Price - Surface Laptop 13-inch starts at $899. - Surface Pro 12-inch starts at $799, making it $100 cheaper. ### Use Case Summary - Choose **Surface Pro** if you need a lighter, more portable 2-in-1 device that doubles as a tablet and supports pen input. - Choose **Surface Laptop** if you want a traditional laptop experience with integrated keyboard and longer battery life, better port selection, and slightly bigger display for productivity. The Surface Pro is ideal for users who prioritize mobility and versatility, while the Surface Laptop targets users who want a powerful, all-day laptop with familiar form factor and connectivity.
